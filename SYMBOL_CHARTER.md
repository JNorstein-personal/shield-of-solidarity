Charter of the Shield of Solidarity


Purpose

The Shield of Solidarity (Against Hate & Narrowmindedness) was created as a visible declaration of allyship with people and communities who are disproportionately targeted by hatred, discrimination, exclusion, violence, or political persecution.

This includes, but is not limited to, members of LGBTQIA+ communities, Black, Indigenous, and other People of Color (BIPOC), neurodivergent people, disabled people, religious minorities, immigrants, and others whose dignity, safety, or equal participation in society is threatened by prejudice or systems of exclusion.

The shield shape is intentional. It represents a willingness to stand alongside those communities and to oppose forces that seek to marginalize, silence, intimidate, or dehumanize them.

The symbol is not intended to speak for any community. Rather, it exists as a public commitment by those who choose to wear, display, reproduce, or share it.

Commitments

Those who choose to display this symbol may do so for many reasons. The creator hopes it will be understood as expressing one or more of the following commitments:

Opposition to hatred, bigotry, discrimination, and dehumanization.

Solidarity with people targeted because of identity, disability, race, ethnicity, nationality, religion, sex, gender, sexuality, or other characteristics that are frequently used as grounds for exclusion or mistreatment.

Recognition that silence and neutrality can enable injustice.

A willingness to participate in building communities characterized by dignity, inclusion, mutual respect, and shared humanity.

Respect for the autonomy, voices, and self-determination of the communities represented by the symbol's visual influences.

A belief that symbols of solidarity should remain accessible to everyone rather than controlled by any individual, organization, or institution.

Stewardship

The Shield of Solidarity was dedicated to the public domain under CC0 1.0 Universal at the time of its publication.

The creator intentionally declined exclusive ownership and invites all people to use, adapt, reproduce, reinterpret, and share the symbol.

Its continued meaning and relevance belong not to its creator alone, but to the communities it was created to support and to the people who choose to stand beside them.
